What Is SA2?

SA2 means Statistical Area Level 2, an official ABS geography used for regional analysis. It usually represents communities with social and economic interaction.

How To Read The Map

  • Darker/brighter fill means higher opportunity score for selected industry.
  • Score combines unmet business supply and local demand indicators.
  • Use filters to narrow to one state, industry, or suburb search.

Metric Glossary

  • Demand index: 0-1 blend of family composition, family income, and one-year growth.
  • Underserved businesses: expected businesses minus observed businesses (floor at zero).
  • Opportunity score: underserved businesses multiplied by demand factor.

Help and Transparency

How this works

The app compares observed business counts to expected business counts for each SA2 and industry, then adjusts by demand signals.

Expected business count is derived from state median business density. Opportunity score is computed as underserved businesses multiplied by a demand factor.

How to use this map
  1. Select an industry layer from the right panel.
  2. Optionally filter by state or territory.
  3. Search an SA2 name if you want a specific location.
  4. Click a map polygon to inspect local metrics and top industry suggestions.
  5. Use bottom charts to validate score evidence.
